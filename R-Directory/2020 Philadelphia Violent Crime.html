Our Data

The data we will explore and utilize for analysis and statistical visualization in our final project will be Philadelphia, Pennsylvania 2020 crime data. Notably, this data was obtained from OpenDataPhilly, which is recognized by and partnered with the City of Philadelphia Office of Innovation & Technology, with the goal to offer official and reliable open source data on city resources available for general access on one site. In regard to data specifications, this crime data contains city-wide geographically and spatially tagged crime incidents reported, catalogued, and organized by the city of Philadelphia Police Department (PPD). Besides where (lat, lng) and when (dispatch_date_time) the crime incidents occur, other attributes include Police Service Area (psa), Police District (dc_district), and legal characterization of incident (text_general code). The PPD classifies the crime incidents in two distinct classifications: Part I crimes & Part II crimes. Part I crime incidents consist of violent offenses: aggravated assault, rape, arson, and other violent offenses. Part II crimes consist of the more non-violent crime incidents: simple assault, prostitution, gambling, fraud, and other non-violent offenses. While the entire data set for crime incidents in the city of Philadelphia catalogue both Part I & Part II crimes dating back to 2006 through present day (updated every minute), our analysis will only focus on violent crimes (Part I crime incidents) for the entire year of 2020. In fact we change header titles and deleted the columns we did not utilize for practical use in R.

Things To Know Before Browsing Visuals

What is Violent Crime?

According to National Institute of Justice: In a violent crime, a victim is harmed by or threatened with violence. Violent crimes include rape and sexual assault, robbery, assault and murder.

What is Assault? What classifies as "Other Assaults"?

Violent crimes includes any type of assault, which means any act attempting or threatening to hurt someone physically. While there may be assault categories that have that are clearly defined such as "Aggravated Assault Firearm" (meaing an aggravated assault commited with a firearm), others are lumped into one generic assault offense type. Hence, "other assaults" includes any action which falls within the definition and is not defined within the other assault categories.
